Research Interest

As Professor Raffaella Margutti said, "All astrophysical phenomena are beautiful." I truly love learning anything that relates to astrophysics. Unfortunately, we all have to choose something to focus on. My interests can change as I am starting off a career in astronomy. Generally, I am interested in the observational side of the field. More specifically, I am interested in studying low-mass stars, brown dwarfs, and exoplanets.

Personal Life

Hello, I am Efrain Alvarado III. A little about me is that I am a first-generation Hispanic student. My academic journey was not so linear. I first started off at San Francisco State University in 2017 but left due to the cost of living. So, I enrolled in a community college the following year, Sacramento City College, where I completed all my breadth/general education requirements in 2021. Finally, I was able to complete my Bachelor's Degree in Astrophysics at UC Berkeley (Go Bears!) in the summer of 2023.

I was fortunate enough to know at a very young age that I wanted to be an astronomer. It was all thanks to my uncle, who used to talk to me about the stars and the planets, which always captivated me. The pivotal moment for me was looking through the telescope and seeing Jupiter and its moons. It was then I realized that this is what Galileo might have seen in 1610. Now, I am on a quest to fulfill that childhood dream and contribute to human knowledge about the universe.

If you find any of my work interesting or would like to connect please reach out to me on the contact page!
